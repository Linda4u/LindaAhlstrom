Chapter 1: BREADS AND PANCAKES

1. Linda’s Rolls

4 cups warm water
2 Tbsp. yeast4 Tbsp. sugar4 tsp. salt10 cups flour

Dissolve yeast in water. Add sugar, salt, and 8 cups of flour, mix well. Dump out on heavily floured board or countertop. Knead dough, adding flour as needed (up to 2 to 3 cups more, depending on the humidity) until smooth and elastic (about 10-15 minutes by hand; 8 minutes with mixer dough hook). Let rise. Form into rolls or loaves (about three or four long, skinny loaves). Place on ungreased baking sheet, sprinkled with corn meal. Bake rolls at 400-425 °F (204-218 °C) for 15 minutes. For shiny surface brush with beaten egg white. Bake 2 minutes more. Bake French bread at 375 °F (191 °C) for 35 minutes.

2. Corn Bread nice and moist

Bake in greased 9 by 9 inch pan, at 400 °F (204 °C) for about 20 minutes.

¾ cup corn meal

1 ¼ cups milk
1 cup white flour1 ½ tsp baking powder

½ tsp salt

1/3 cup sugar1 egg

¼ cup vegetable oil

Preheat oven to 400 °F (204 °C).

In a small bowl, stir together the cornmeal and milk.

In a large bowl, mix flour, baking powder, salt and sugar. Add egg, oil and cornmeal mixture. Stir just to moisten, dump into greased 9X9” pan, and bake for 20 minutes or until toothpick inserted in center comes out clean.

3. Big Daddy Biscuits

2 cups flour
1 Tbsp. baking powder1 tsp. salt1 Tbsp. sugar1/3 cup shortening or butter1 cup milk

Sift and mix together the dry ingredients. Cut shortening into flour mixture until size of peas. Add milk and mix briefly just until moistened. Roll out on lightly floured surface. Cut into rounds. (cut off both ends of a can to make a biscuit cutter.) Place on cookie sheet. Bake at 425 °F (218 °C) for 13-15 minutes.

4. Pancakes

2 Tbsp. “Harina Gran” con Granos enteros

½ cup whole wheat flour

½ cup white flour

2 Tbsp. corn meal or harina de corn (corn flour)

¾ tsp baking powder

1/8 tsp baking soda

½ tsp salt

½ Tbsp. sugar

1 Tbsp dry milk powder1 egg, extra large, beaten1 cup water1 tsp vinegar1 Tbsp salad oil

Mix dry ingredients together in large bowl. Mix wet ingredients together in small bowl, and add to flour mixture, without over mixing.

Pour about ¼ cup portions onto a hot griddle. Bake until bubbles pop and stay popped, turn and bake until golden brown. Serve hot, with butter and syrup on top.

5. German Pancake Breakfast Casserole

¾ cup butter

4-5 large apples, sliced into thin wedges, with peel on.6 Tbsp. sugar1 tsp cinnamon2 Tbsp. lemon juice8 eggs1 1/3 cups flour1 1/3 cups milk4 Tbsp. butter, melted

Grated rind of 1 lemon

Heat oven to 425 °F (218 °C). Melt butter in 9X13 inch casserole dish. Add apples, sugar, cinnamon, and lemon juice and put in oven until apples are tender (about 20 minutes). Mix rest of ingredients in a blender until smooth; pour over apples. Bake 30 minutes, or until knife inserted in center comes out clean; should look golden brown and puffy.

Chapter 2: CAKES AND COOKIES

1. Picnic Cake Darlene Alder

Soak 1 cup chopped dates in 1 ½ cups hot water; cool. Add and mix:

¾ cup shortening

1 cup sugar
2 eggs1 tsp. baking soda1 tsp. cinnamon

½ tsp. salt

1 tsp. vanilla1 ½ cup + 2 Tbsp. flour

Pour mixture into greased 9X13 inch pan. Mix and sprinkle on top of batter: ½ cup brown sugar, ½ cup chopped nuts 1 cup chocolate chips. Bake at 350 °F (177 °C) for 40 minutes: serve warm or cold.

2. Carrot Cake

Mix the following five ingredients well:

3 cups grated carrots
4 eggs2 cups sugar1 ½ cups salad oil1 tsp. vanilla

Sift together the following four ingredients:

1 tsp. baking soda

½ tsp. salt

2 cups flour1 tsp. cinnamon

Add 1 cup chopped nuts to the dry ingredients.

Combine the mixtures and beat well. Pour into greased 9X13 inch pan and bake in preheated oven at 350 °F (177 °C) for 40-45 minutes.

3. Moist Apple Cake

2 ½ cups flour
2 cups sugar2 tsp. baking powder1 tsp. baking soda1 tsp. salt1 tsp. cinnamon2 eggs

¾ cup unsweetened applesauce

¼ cup canola oil

1 tsp. vanilla extract3 cups chopped peeled tart apples (3 medium)

½ cup chopped nuts

Sift the first six ingredients together into a large bowl. In a small bowl beat the eggs; blend in the applesauce, oil, and vanilla. Stir into the flour mixture; mix well. Fold in the chopped apples and nuts.

Pour into a well greased 9X13 inch pan. Bake at 350 °F (177 °C) for 40-45 minutes or until toothpick inserted in center comes out clean.

Apple Cake SAUCE:

3 Tbsp. butter1 cup brown sugar1/3 cup milk1 tsp. vanilla extract

In a small pan, melt butter; add the other 3 ingredients. Bring to a boil, stirring constantly. Pour over warm cake. Serve warm. Yield: 15 servings.

4. KAREN’S BROWNIES

1 cup butter

¾ cup cocoa

2 cups sugar2 tsp. vanilla3 eggs1 ½ cups flour1 cup chopped nuts

Preheat oven to 350 °F (177 °C). Melt butter and cocoa in pan. Set aside. Mix sugar, vanilla, eggs, flour and nuts (or reserve nuts to sprinkle on top of frosting). Add chocolate mixture. Put in greased 9X13 inch pan. Bake at 350 °F (177 °C) for 25-30 minutes. Check with toothpick. Sprinkle with powdered sugar or frost with fudge frosting.

6. GINGERSNAPS – Yield: 3 dozen cookies

¾ cup butter

1 cup white sugar
1 egg

¼ cup molasses

2 cups flour1 tsp. ginger1 tsp. cinnamon2 tsp. baking soda

½ tsp. salt

½ cup white sugar (reserved – to coat)

Preheat oven to 350 °F (177 °C). In medium bowl, cream together butter and 1 cup white sugar until smooth. Beat in egg and molasses until well blended. Combine flour, ginger, cinnamon, baking soda and salt; stir into molasses mixture to form dough. Roll dough into one inch balls and roll the balls in the reserved sugar. Place cookies 2 inches apart onto ungreased cookie sheets. Bake for 8-10 minutes in the preheated oven 350 °F (177 °C). Allow cookies to cool on baking sheet for 5 minutes before removing to a wire rack to cool completely.

7. OATMEAL COOKIES Priel Davis

1 cup butter
4 cups oatmeal2 cups sugar2 cups milk2 eggs, beaten4 cups flour4 tsp. baking powder1 tsp. salt1 lb. raisins

Melt butter; add oatmeal, sugar, and milk; stir. Add beaten eggs; stir. Stir in rest of ingredients. (I like to add 1 tsp. vanilla, and 1 tsp. cinnamon.) Bake at 375 °F (191 °C) for 10 minutes.

8. PUMPKIN CHOCOLATE CHIP COOKIES (or raisin cookies)

½ cup butter

1 ½ cups brown sugar

½ cup white sugar

2 eggs1 tsp. vanilla1 ½ cup pumpkin puree (or winter squash puree)2 ½ cups flour

¼ tsp. baking soda

2 tsp. baking powder

9. SIERRA NUGGETS Fred Reiche

½ cup butter, soft

½ cup brown sugar

¾ cup white sugar

1 ½ tsp. milk

¾ tsp. vanilla

1 egg1 ½ cups oatmeal1 cup cornflakes (or increase oatmeal to 2 cups)

¾ cup flour

1 1/8 tsp. baking soda

½ tsp salt

¼ tsp. mace

¾ tsp. cinnamon

1/8 tsp. nutmeg

Pinch of ground cloves

2 oz. coconut flakes or ½ to 1 cup grated dry coconut1 cup raisins or chocolate chips

½ cup walnuts, chopped

Cream together butter and sugars. Add vanilla and egg; beat well. Stir in oatmeal and cereal flakes. Sift together flour, soda, salt and spices. Add to creamed mixture and mix thoroughly. Stir in coconut, raisins or chips, and nuts. Drop by well rounded spoonfuls onto greased cookie sheet. Bake in preheated oven 350 °F (177 °C) for 10 minutes. Yield 4 dozen cookies.

Chapter 3. OTHER DESSERTS

1. Apple Crisp

¼ cup butter

¼ cup chopped walnuts

Mix apples, sugar and lemon juice together. Place in 9X9 inch pan. Mix dry ingredients and butter together; sprinkle on top of apples. Bake in preheated oven at 375 °F (191 °C) for 30-35 minutes.

2. APPLE PIE

Crust:

1 ¼ cup flour
1 Tbsp. sugar

¼ tsp. salt

8 Tbsp. (1/4 lb. or 113 grams) cold butter, cut into ¼ inch cubes3 Tbsp. very cold water

Mix flour, sugar, salt and butter with your fingers. Add the cold water, stirring lightly with a fork. Form into ball and roll out on floured surface. Place in pie pan (or non-stick frying pan); cut off excess dough. Roll out and cut strips for criss cross crust.

Filling:

4 large apples, sliced

½ tsp nutmeg

1 ½ tsp. cinnamon

¾ cup sugar

2 Tbsp. flour

Mix dry ingredients together; gently mix with apples. Place in unbaked crust. Add strips to form criss cross crust; pinch edge of crusts together. Bake in preheated oven at 375 °F (191 °C) about 1 hour.

3. CHEESECAKE – from Dee Moon

1 graham cracker crust
1 – 8 oz package softened cream cheese1 can sweetened condensed milk1/3 cup lemon juice1 tsp. vanilla

Beat milk and cream cheese first and then beat in lemon juice and vanilla. Pour into crust, refrigerate and let set up for several hours. Before serving, top with cherry or lemon pie filling.

4. Brownie Trifle with Strawberries made with CHEWY BROWNIES

½ tsp. baking soda

¾ cup cocoa powder

½ cup boiling water

2 cup sugar
2 eggs

1/3 cup canola oil1 1/3 cup flour1 tsp. vanilla

¼ tsp. salt

Combine soda and cocoa. Add boiling water, stir. Beat together oil, sugar, eggs; add to cocoa mixture and blend. Add flour, vanilla and salt; stir, just until smooth. Spread batter in greased 9X13 inch pan. Bake in preheated oven at 350 °F (177 °C) for 15 minutes. Allow to cool completely. Cut into 1X1 inch chunks. Make vanilla pudding; cool. Wash, hull, and slice strawberries. Put 4-5 brownie chunks in bottom of individual cups; top with 1/3 cup pudding and some fresh strawberry slices. Add a drizzle of chocolate syrup. Add a dollop (heaping spoonful) of sweetened whipped cream (Chantilly). Add a few more strawberry slices. Refrigerate until ready to serve.

6. CHOCOLATE PUDDING MIX – Jean Davis

1 ½ cup sugar
1 ½ cups powdered milk1 ¼ cups flour1 tsp. salt

¾ cup cocoa

10 cups hot water4 Tbsp. butter4 eggs, beaten4 tsp. vanilla

Sift dry ingredients and stir together. Heat water to just below boiling point. Whisk in the dry mixture; stir constantly until pudding starts to boil. Remove from heat. Gradually add hot pudding to eggs, beating constantly; drizzling in all 3 cups of the hot pudding. Mix and pour this back into the pan of hot pudding. Return to heat for one minute; stirring constantly. Remove from heat; add butter and vanilla; stir. Serve warm or cold. Henry likes it best hot or warm with a scoop of ice cream on top.

1 pound chicken breasts, cut up
1 medium onion, chopped1 Tbsp. olive oil2 garlic cloves, minced2 cans (14 oz each) chicken broth (or your own chicken broth)1 can chopped green chilies (or chopped chilies according to your taste)2 tsp. ground cumin2 tsp. dried oregano1 ½ tap. 5. CHICKEN BROCCOLI CASSEROLE

2 cups chicken, cooked (roasted, cut in chunks)
3 cups broccoli2 slices of cheese cut into ¼ inch cubes1 Tbsp. lemon juice1 Tbsp. parmesan cheese

¼ cup celery tops, leaves cut in thin slices

2 Tbsp. chicken fat or butter2 Tbsp. flour

½ tsp. salt

1 ½ cups milk

½ cup chicken broth

½ cup bread crumbs

1 Tbsp. butter1/3 cup parmesan cheese

Layer chicken, broccoli and cheese. Sprinkle lemon juice and parmesan cheese over top. Make white sauce: sauté celery tops in fat, add flour and salt. Gradually add milk and broth; cook over medium heat until thickened, stirring constantly. Pour over top of chicken and broccoli. Top with buttered crumbs, mixed with parmesan cheese. Bake at 350 °F (177 °C) for 20 to 25 minutes. [Sometimes I start the casserole with a layer of cooked brown rice – 2 to 3 cups.]

6. DAVY CROCKETT’S CHICKEN AND BISCUITS

1 chicken, cut up
3 cups chicken broth1 ½ cups sliced celery1 onion, chopped2 cups sliced carrots1/3 cup margarine or rendered chicken fat

½ cup flour

1 tsp. poultry seasoning*1 ½ cups milk

Put chicken, broth, onion, celery, carrots in large kettle. Bring to boil, simmer until chicken is tender. Remove chicken; cool and debone, Set aside. In saucepan melt margarine, stir in flour and poultry seasoning. Remove from heat, gradually add 2 cups broth and milk; blend well. Bring to boil stirring constantly. Boil gently 1 minute; add chicken and vegetables. Place in 9X13 inch dish.

Use your own biscuit recipe or the following:

1 cup flour

½ cup shredded cheese

1 ½ tsp. baking powder

¼ tsp. salt

3 Tbsp. butter or margarine

½ cup milk

Combine flour, cheese, baking powder and salt. Cut in margarine. Add milk, stir gently. Drop biscuits by rounded tablespoon on hot chicken-vegetable mixture. Bake in preheated oven at 425 °F (218 °C) for 20-30 minutes

*Homemade poultry seasoning using ground dried spices: 2 tsp. sage, 1 ½ tsp. thyme, 1 tsp. marjoram, 3.4 tsp. rosemary, ½ tsp. nutmeg, ½ tsp. black pepper. Combine and store in sealed container. (Spanish names for spices: salvia, tomillo, mejorana, romero, nuez moscada, pimiento negro.)

7. LASAGNE

1 lb. ground beef, browned and seasoned with salt, chopped onion and minced garlic

½ cup parmesan cheese

8. MEXICAN BEEF LASAGNE Susan Rasmussen

1 Tbsp. olive oil
1 medium onion, finely chopped600g lean ground beef8 corn tortillas

Mexican seasoning

Mild Mexican salsa

400g can red kidney beans, drained, rinsed2 medium tomatoes, finely chopped

¼ cup pickled jalapeno chilies, drained, chopped

1 1/3 cups grated cheese

Guacamole, sliced red chili and lime wedges

Lightly grease a 6cm deep, 20cm (base) square ovenproof dish. Heat oil in a large frying pan over medium-high heat. Add onion. Cook, stirring, for 3 minutes or until soft. Add meat. Cook, stirring with a wooden spoon to break up mince, for 4 minutes or until browned. Add seasoning, beans and 1/3 cup cold water. Cook, stirring, for 2-3 minutes or until mixture has thickened. Place 2 tortillas, trimming to fit in prepared dish. Spread one-third of meat mixture over tortillas. Sprinkle with one-third of the tomato and chilies. Sprinkle with 1/3 cup cheese. Repeat layers, ending with tortillas. Spread salsa over tortillas. Sprinkle with remaining cheese. Bake at 400 °F (204 °C) for 20 to 25 minutes or until golden. Set aside for 10 minutes. Cut into pieces. Serve with guacamole, chili and lime wedges.

Chapter 5. SOUPS, SAUCES and SALADS

1. CHICKEN SOUP

Boil bones of 1 chicken with water, celery, onion, salt and Monterrey Steak Seasoning. Strain the liquid. To the broth, add carrots, white beans with juice, and some yellow chili peppers.

POTATO SOUP

1 medium onion
5 large potatoes, peeled, cut in chunks2 Tbsp. butter1 tsp. salt1 Tbsp. parsley

¼ tsp. pepper

1 (12 oz) can evaporated milk (or 1/2 cup dry milk powder mixed with 1 ½ cups water)

Sauté onion in butter. Add potatoes, salt, and pepper. Cover with water; bring to boiling and simmer until potatoes are soft. Without draining water, mash potatoes. Add parsley and evaporated milk.

1 cup tomatoes, cut in chunks
1 leek, sliced (or sweet onion)2 (14 oz) cans palmitos (palm hearts1 avocado, cut in ½ inch pieces

¼ cup fresh parsley, chopped

½ tsp. lime zest

2 Tbsp. lime juice2 Tbsp. mayonnaise2 Tbsp. canola oil

Put the tomatoes, onion, palm hearts (cut in ½ inch chunks), avocado and parsley in a medium bowl. Blend the lime zest, lime juice, mayonnaise, oil, salt and pepper; pour over vegetables and gently mix. Serve chilled. Try adding zucchini! Also good with a dash or two of Merkén seasoning.
